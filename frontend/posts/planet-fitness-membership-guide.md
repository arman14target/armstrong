Planet Fitness is the most-searched budget gym brand in the United States — famous for the **$10/month** Classic membership and the **Black Card** tier with guest passes and massage chairs. With **2,500+ clubs** across North America and growing international franchises, it dominates "cheap gym near me" searches. This guide breaks down **real pricing**, **where clubs are located**, and **what equipment you can expect** before you sign up.

## Planet Fitness Membership Tiers & Pricing (2026)

> **Pricing note:** All costs below are **approximate estimates** for 2026 based on publicly listed rates, member reports, and typical promo pricing. Your actual price can differ by location, contract length, annual fee (~$49/year), enrollment fees, and seasonal promotions. Always confirm current rates at your local club before signing.

| Tier | Est. Monthly Cost | What You Get |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Classic** | ~$10–$15/mo | Home club only, basic cardio + strength floor |
| **Black Card** | ~$24.99–$29.99/mo | All clubs nationwide, guest privileges, HydroMassage, tanning*, total body enhancement* |
| **Black Card Plus** | ~$34.99/mo (select markets) | Black Card perks + premium amenities where offered |

\*Tanning and total body enhancement availability varies by state and club.

### Hidden Costs to Budget For

- **Annual fee** — typically ~$39–$49, billed once per year (often in March or October)
- **Startup/enrollment fee** — frequently waived during promos; otherwise ~$49–$99
- **Commitment** — most clubs offer no-commitment month-to-month; 12-month plans may discount the rate

### Classic vs Black Card: Which Is Worth It?

Choose **Classic** if you train at one location, never need guests, and skip recovery amenities.

Choose **Black Card** if you travel and want **any-club access**, bring a partner via guest pass (~unlimited at most clubs), or use **HydroMassage** and massage chairs after leg day.

## Equipment at Planet Fitness

Planet Fitness markets itself as a **"Judgment Free Zone"** for beginners — not a powerlifting dungeon. Equipment varies by club age and size, but most locations follow a similar template.

### Cardio Floor

- Treadmills, ellipticals, stair climbers, bikes
- Arc trainers and rowers at newer builds
- Usually the largest zone in the building

### Strength Area

- **Smith machines** — primary barbell option (no free Olympic barbells for bench/squat at most clubs)
- **Dumbbells** — typically capped around **60–75 lbs** per hand; many clubs stop lower
- Selectorized machines — leg press, lat pulldown, cable crossover, chest press
- Fixed barbell racks — usually light to moderate weights
- Resistance bands and mats for stretching

### What Planet Fitness Usually Does NOT Have

- Power racks or squat racks with free barbells
- Heavy dumbbells (80 lb+)
- Olympic lifting platforms
- Chalk-friendly deadlift zones

Serious strength athletes often outgrow Planet Fitness within a year. Beginners, casual cardio members, and budget-conscious lifters doing machine and Smith work fit the model well.

### Black Card Recovery Zone

- HydroMassage beds
- Massage chairs
- Tanning beds (where legally offered)
- Total body enhancement booths (red-light style, select clubs)

## FAQ: Planet Fitness

### How much is Planet Fitness per month?

**Classic** runs ~$10–$15/month plus annual fee. **Black Card** runs ~$25–$30/month with multi-club access and perks.

### Can you bench press at Planet Fitness?

Yes — on **Smith machines** or dumbbells. Most clubs do not have a standard free bench press station with an Olympic bar.

### Does Planet Fitness have a free trial?

Many clubs offer **free day passes** or discounted first month via the website. Policies vary by franchise owner.

### What is the Planet Fitness lunk alarm?

A novelty policy discouraging excessive grunting or dropping weights. Rarely enforced aggressively, but signals the beginner-friendly culture.

### Is Planet Fitness open 24 hours?

Many US locations operate **24/7**; some close overnight. Check your local club hours online.
